Closed Beta Playbook

A 2–4 week closed beta exists to answer two questions no code can: (1) will people tolerate the verification friction? (2) is “verified effort” actually trustworthy, or gameable? Everything here serves those two.

1 · Who to recruit

  • 15–30 people - small enough to talk to each one, big enough to see a curve.
  • Bias toward people who already want this - fitness / reading / self-improvement types. The beta tests whether the people most likely to love it stick, not whether you can convert skeptics.
  • Mix of devices - some iPhone (they hit the “Add to Home Screen” path), some Android/desktop.
  • Avoid favor-doers who sign up once to be nice - they pollute your retention signal.
Recruiting line: “I built a self-improvement app that works like an RPG - real quests you have to prove you did (photo/timer/reading checks), levels, streaks, a leaderboard. Want to use it for 2 weeks and tell me where it’s annoying or broken? Free, ~2 min/day.”

2 · What to tell testers

  • “It’s early. Things will break - tell me when they do.” Turns bugs into contributions, not disappointments.
  • “Aim for one quest a day. Don’t binge.” The daily habit is the behavior you’re testing.
  • “Be honest in the app - it’s built to catch fakes, and the leaderboard only means something if everyone’s real.”
  • iPhone users: walk them through Add to Home Screen on day 1, or they get no notifications.

Decision rule: strong D2 (>40%) + clean integrity = you have a product, scale recruiting. People bouncing at the first photo = a friction problem to solve before launch - don’t fix it by adding features.

4 · How to collect feedback

  • In-app feedback + Lumi - in-the-moment beats a survey later.
  • One group chat (Discord/WhatsApp) with all testers - highest-value channel; they bond as a cohort, which itself boosts retention.
  • One 10-min call with 3–5 of them mid-beta. Watch them use it without helping.
  • The one survey question (end of week 2): “How would you feel if you could no longer use Ascend?” - >40% “very disappointed” = product-market-fit signal.

5 · Timeline

  • Day 0: onboard each tester personally; get their first quest done while you watch/chat.
  • Days 1–7: mostly observe. Fix only show-stoppers - collect signal before redesigning.
  • Day 7: read D2/D7. Talk to the quiet ones (silence is data).
  • Days 8–14: ship fixes to the top 3 friction points; see if retention responds.
  • Day 14: run the Sean-Ellis question. Decide: scale, iterate, or pivot.

6 · Known risks to watch

  • Anti-cheat is advisory, not bulletproof. On-device AI + liveness code + GPS catch casual fakes; a determined tester can bypass scene detection. If integrity is what people love, harden it server-side later.
  • Rewards are “coming soon.” Make sure testers know Aether is for cosmetics + bragging rights right now.
  • Empty social. Under ~30 people the leaderboard/guild are quiet - the founding-member framing helps; don’t over-index on social this round.
  • iOS push needs the Home-Screen install. “No notifications” on iPhone is almost always the missing install step.

7 · Post-beta: risk-based verification

Verification should be lenient by default, strict only where the stakes are real - the way real anti-fraud works (banks don’t friction every coffee, only the suspicious 1%). Tightening the everyday check just re-breaks honest users; false-rejection of real effort is the #1 retention killer, so minimize it ruthlessly.

Keep the appearance of rigor (burned-in liveness code, “anti-cheat” badge, foreground-locked timers) - that feeling of accountability does ~90% of the work at near-zero friction. For a self-improvement app, verification’s real job is manufacturing a moment of intentionality, not perfect fraud detection: a cheater mostly deceives themselves.

Concentrate strictness on the two places with something to gain:

  • Reward redemption - gated on Integrity. Real rewards require an Integrity score of 80+; flagged submissions drop Integrity below the line, so cheating for a prize is self-defeating. When redemption goes live, enforce this server-side in the redemption function, not just the UI. Cheating for rewards is the single biggest business risk - this neutralizes it.
  • Top of the leaderboard - the few competing for #1 are where a stricter check (a real server-side vision pass, or manual spot-check) is worth the cost.

Everywhere else: stay lenient. Watch the false-rejection rate like a hawk; treat “cheat-through rate” as a later, surgical problem - never solved by punishing the honest majority.

The one-sentence version: get 20 people who want this to use it for two weeks; watch whether they come back on day 2 and whether anyone games the verification - those two answers tell you if you’re ready to launch.
